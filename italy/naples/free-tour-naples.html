Naples is Italy's undiscovered gem.

Where cities like Florence, Venice and Rome are often overrun with tourists, Naples has kept its original charm. Besides this, it has attractions that can rival with all these cities. Naples used to be the capital of a powerful kingdom until very recently and it has kept its regal status. There are royal palaces to be visited and churches worth of kings and queens. The museums are filled with important artworks. But, more importantly, Naples is fun. Wandering through the small streets of the city, you will feel like you have traveled back in time.

What better way to discover this amazing city, than joining one or more of our free tours given in Naples? Our tour guides will show you all the highlights and will make you feel part of the city. Our free walking tours are the best way to discover the amazing city of Naples.

Discover Naples
Here you can find some tips on what to see and do in Naples



What is the best time to visit Naples?

Because of its Mediterranean climate, Naples can be visited every time of the year. Spring and autumn are the best times to visit the city, but even summer and winter bring their own advantages. The winters are not cold and you will encounter a lot less tourists. Naples is lovely during Christmas. The Napolitan presepe, the nativity scenes are world famous. Summers are warm, but you can always find shelter in the shade of the small streets.

What are some of the highlights of Naples?

Naples used to be the capital of a kingdom until 1870. This is still clearly visible. There are so many palaces, churches and museums. There are two royal palaces. Both are museums these days. Especially the Museo di Capodimonte is worth a visit. In this royal palace you can find the art collection of the kings of Naples and Sicily. The Spaccanapoli is the long street that divides the center of Naples. This street has always kept is charming character. It's one of the best places to buy traditional Napolitan goods, like limoncello, sfogliatelle and Christmas decorations.The archeological museum is world class. Here you find the most important discoveries from Pompeii and much more. Another fun thing to do is to take a funicolare up to the Castel Sant'Elmo for one of the most beautiful views of the bay of Naples, with Vesuvius in the background. On one of our free tours, our guides will show you all the best places to go in the center of Naples and take you through the small streets to see what makes Naples great for yourself.

What books or websites can I read to prepare me for my stay in Naples

In the Shadow of Vesuvius by Jordan Lancaster is a good read. Eyewitness top 10 Naples & Amalfi coast is a good guide book. Lonely planets Southern Italy has a good section about Naples too.
